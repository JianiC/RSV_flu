Note there is no nclimdivi data for the states of Hawaii and Alaska. Values have been downloaded for entire state area averaged. Data is available at the division level based on NOAA US climate divisions. Use the link above to access portal to download time-series data. 

The following values were downloaded for each U.S. State:

Temperature(F)
Precipitation(Inches)
Palmer Drought index
Maximum Temperature (F)
Minimum Temperature (F)
Heating Degree Days (HDD)
Cooling Degree Days (CDD)



Heating degree days (HDD) are a measure of how cold the temperature was on a given day or during a period of days (ela.gov)

Cooling degree days (CDD) are a measure of how hot the temperature was on a given day or during a period of days.(ela.gov)


Additionally, the average across the seasons of interest can be extracted using the seasonal average option. For the typical influenza season the average from the month of September to the Month of April. For example, if listed for 2020, this average is for September 2019 to April 2020.
